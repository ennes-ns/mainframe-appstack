# RPGBOT Bard 5e Class Guide (Tier List)

## What is a DnD Bard?
The Bard is fantastically versatile. With access to every skill, expertise, full casting, and a decent set of proficiencies, the Bard can fill essentially every role in the party. Subclasses like **College of Lore** are more of the classic support Bard, with improved magical options and support abilities, while **College of Swords** and **College of Valor** can serve as front-line melee characters who can bring their spellcasting and support capabilities into the heat of battle.

Because bards can do so much within a single class, they can fill nearly any role within a party. Their open skill options, Expertise, and buff spells allow them to thrive as a **Face** and a **Scout**, while their offensive spellcasting makes them effective **Controllers** and **Support** casters. The Bard’s biggest deficiencies are damage output and durability, so it’s hard to build them as a **Blaster**, **Defender**, or **Striker**, but that’s not to say that it’s impossible.

Strangely, the Bard’s emphasis on the **Performance** skill from previous editions has wholly vanished. There is nearly no mechanical reason to take the skill. That’s not to say that your DM can’t make it fun, of course. If nothing else, if your game uses the Lifestyle rules or the Downtime rules, your proficiency in Performance allows you to live comfortably.

## Bard Class Features

*   **Hit Points:** **d8** is fantastic for a full casting class, but you’re not going to survive rushing into melee all the time unless you go for Valor to boost your AC.
*   **Saves:** **Dexterity** is great for avoiding fireballs, but most spells which call for Reflex saves won’t outright disable you, and **Charisma** saves are extremely rare.
*   **Proficiencies:** Light armor and a handful of weapons won’t give you a ton of options, but it’s enough to get by, and Bards rely mostly on their spells and special abilities. You do get three skills of your choice, which opens up a lot of really great options.

### 1: Spellcasting
The Bard is a full caster like a Cleric or Wizard, and casts spells based on their Charisma. Bards use a “spells known” mechanic similar to a Sorcerer, so your abilities are limited to the spells you know. You can replace one spell known every level, so don’t worry if you choose a spell at low level and it doesn’t remain useful as you gain levels.

### 1: Bardic Inspiration
It’s tempting to throw this up before every fight, but since the duration is only 10 minutes and you only get a handful of uses per day, it’s important to be conservative with them. When you know that your party needs a bit of help, like on a difficult save or on a crucial attack roll, give them an inspiration die. Since it can be used after rolling the d20, the inspiration die can be a fantastic option when you’re a point or two short of a save DC or your enemy’s AC. At fifth level the uses recharge on a Short Rest (see “Font of Inspiration”), so you can afford to be much less stingy, but you still don’t want to burn through them too quickly.

> **Bardic Inspiration:** You can inspire others through stirring words or music. To do so, you use a bonus action on your turn to choose one creature other than yourself within 60 feet of you who can hear you. That creature gains one Bardic Inspiration die, a d6... Your Bardic Inspiration die changes when you reach certain levels in this class. The die becomes a d8 at 5th level, a d10 at 10th level, and a d12 at 15th level.

### 2: Jack of All Trades
Jack of All Trades may be one of the most easily-overlooked class features in the game. The obvious function is that it adds to all of your skill checks where you’re not already proficient, but it also applies to a dizzying long list of other ability checks.

**Examples where Jack of All Trades applies:**
*   Initiative
*   Counterspell / Dispel Magic
*   Constitution checks (Hold breath, labor, etc.)
*   Intelligence check to escape the Maze spell

### 3: Song of Rest
A little of healing is always nice. Your allies need to regain hit points at the end of the rest involving hit dice to get the extra 1d6.

### 3: Bard College
*   **College of Creation:** Sing objects to life.
*   **College of Eloquence:** Reinforces core features.
*   **College of Glamour:** Support and Charm.
*   **College of Lore:** Extra Expertise and Magical Secrets.
*   **College of Spirits:** Commune with the dead.
*   **College of Swords:** Flourishes / Martial.
*   **College of Valor:** Medium armor / Extra Attack.
*   **College of Whispers:** Intrigue / Deception.

### 3: Expertise
Choose two of your skill proficiencies. Your proficiency bonus is doubled. (Again at 10th level).

### 5: Font of Inspiration
Regain all uses of Bardic Inspiration on a short or long rest.

### 6: Countercharm
Situational. Advantage on saves against being frightened or charmed.

### 10, 14, 18: Magical Secrets
Plunder magic from any class.

### 20: Superior Inspiration
Regain one use of Bardic Inspiration if you have none left when rolling initiative.

## What Ability Scores are Good for the Bard?

*   **Str:** **Dump stat.** Even in melee, the Bard can rely solely on Dexterity.
*   **Dex:** **Important.** Boosts your poor AC and weapon attacks.
*   **Con:** **Important.** HP and Concentration saves.
*   **Int:** Only useful for specific knowledge skills.
*   **Wis:** Good for saves and Perception.
*   **Cha:** **Primary.** The Bard runs on Charisma. Maximize this first.

---

## What Skills are Good for the Bard?
*   **Acrobatics (Dex):** Situational / Escape grapples.
*   **Arcana (Int):** Important Knowledge.
*   **Deception (Cha):** **Face** skill.
*   **Insight (Wis):** **Face** skill.
*   **Intimidation (Cha):** **Face** skill.
*   **Investigation (Int):** Important.
*   **Perception (Wis):** **Crucial.** Most rolled skill.
*   **Performance (Cha):** Thematic, but mechanically weak in 5e.
*   **Persuasion (Cha):** **King of Face skills.**
*   **Religion (Int):** Important Knowledge.
*   **Stealth (Dex):** Essential for scouts.

---

## What Backgrounds are Good for the Bard?
*   **Acolyte:** Languages and decent skills.
*   **Charlatan:** Good for stealthy/social Bards.
*   **Cloistered Scholar:** Great for Lore Bards.
*   **Criminal:** Essential if replacing a Rogue.
*   **Entertainer:** Conceptually fits, but mechanically poor.

---

## What Feats are Good for the Bard?
*   **Actor:** Great for social games.
*   **Fey Touched:** **Blue.** Misty Step + 1st level spell.
*   **Inspiring Leader:** **Green.** Huge pool of Temp HP.
*   **Lucky:** Good on anyone.
*   **Metamagic Adept:** Excellent modification options.
*   **Resilient (Con):** **Blue.** Essential for Concentration.
*   **Skill Expert:** More Expertise is always good.
*   **War Caster:** Great for melee Bards.

---

## What Weapons/Armor are Good for the Bard?
*   **Rapier:** Best melee option.
*   **Light Crossbow:** Best ranged option.
*   **Studded Leather:** Standard armor.
*   **Half Plate / Shield:** Best for Valor Bards.

---

## What Multiclassing Options are Good for the Bard?
*   **Fighter:** Proficiency boost (Lore Bards).
*   **Paladin:** Divine Smite (2-level dip).
*   **Rogue:** Skill machine (1-level dip).
*   **Warlock:** **Hexblade** is a classic 1-level dip for bards.

---

## What Magic Items are Good for the Bard?
*   **Common:** Instrument of Illusions, Masquerade Tattoo.
*   **Uncommon:** Cloak of Protection, **Instrument of the Bards**, Stone of Good Luck.
*   **Rare:** Amulet of Health, Mantle of Spell Resistance, Reveler’s Concertina.
*   **Very Rare:** Animated Shield, **Rhythm-Maker’s Drum +3**.
*   **Legendary:** Cloak of Invisibility, Tome of Leadership and Influence.
